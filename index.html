About VOW

Valley OF Welfare (VOW) is Community Based Organization founded in 2016. Led by Youth who are very committed to a continual and highly sprited national eradication of illiteracy among the elderly as well as the young.
We empower the young and the elderly, both in and out of school with relative skills, insights and opportunities they need to get education and realize their dreams. ...

We do this through a series of programs and activities that ensure initiation and sustainability of literacy programs,advocacy program and community's transformational projects that aim to mitigate the impact of illiteracy in the society such as poverty.
The mission and work of the Valley Of Welfare are guided by the purposes and principles contained in its founding Charter.

VIEWPOINT

In an age of economic boom and scientific prosperity, isn't the idea that there exist people incapable yet of enjoying the perks such prosperity brings, or rather unable to make the most out of it just like others, tragic? Or perhaps the fact there exist others who, as much as they, would want to talk about and address the challenges that face them, still can't do? The strangest thing though being there is hardly any injustice and oppression involved. The biggest factor in play simply being an inability to express their views in a language the concerned party understands. Yes, that's right, a simple language barrier. Here is the twist in the story though; Mostly it is purely circumstantial.
"Knowledge is power. Information is progress. Education is the premise of progress, in every society, in every family.”- Kofi Annan.
It is out this despondent reality, that we have initiated and started championing for these programs aimed at eradication of illiteracy and tirelessly working towards achieving an educated society, for this generation and those to come.

Our services

Education

The primary purpose of adult education is to provide a second chance for those who have lost access to education for other reasons in order to achieve social justice and equal access to education.

Youth Empowerment

Through educational forums, seminars, open mic events and such like programs, we avail to the youth a platform through which they nuture and showcase their talents

Improving life standards

We do this through sponsoring needy students and orphans. Getting an education is a permanent solution to extreme poverty particularly the kind many orphans face every day.

Which side of you ?

1) If you discover something that you’re passionate about, pursue it. Pursue it with everything you have.
2) If you need to cry, cry. Let tears run down your face until there are no more left, until you’re sure you can never cry again. Then wipe your cheeks, and continue fighting.
3) Take time for yourself. Sometimes when everything in your head gets too crowded and confusing, you just need to shut yourself out, to hole up in your room and just be alone with your thoughts.
4. Be nice to your parents. If they’re angry with you, it’s most likely because they’re worried. And think back, maybe you mistreated them. Maybe you weren’t fair. They’re the reason you’re in this world in the first place, so treat them accordingly.
5. Your heart will get broken, there’s no need to lie. But hearts don’t stay that way. It doesn’t matter if someone comes along and fixes it for you or if you fix it yourself. It’s not what you want to hear, but the boy who crushes your heart into tiny pieces probably won’t even matter to you five years from now.
6. Cut toxic people out of your life. You don’t need them. Don’t let them take your happiness.
7. It’s not selfish to put yourself first from time to time. Not at all.
8. Don’t ever wonder if you’re good enough. Instead try to be the best version of yourself. If you think you are not good enough for someone, think again: let go of them. They don’t deserve you, not the other way around.
9. Losing someone you love is always hard, no matter the circumstances. “Time heals wounds” is not necessarily true - but time helps. Time lets the pain fade to a dull ache in your chest until you wake up one day and you’re okay.
10. Don’t worry too much. Easy to say, isn’t it? What I mean is: don’t worry about things you don’t have to worry about. Because you don’t have to spend your days dreading the next week or dreading your life after school. There’s no point, it only drags you down.
11. If someone says “you will never make it” to you, smile at them and try as hard as you can to get where you want to be. You don’t have to show them anything, but it still feels fucking fantastic to prove them wrong in the end, not gonna lie.
12. Make other people happy. Yes. How good does it feel to make someone smile? Tell your best friend she looks amazing when you meet her, help your mum do the dishes, smile at strangers on your way home. Make yourself feel better by simply being a good person.
13. Learn how to forgive others. It’s a blessing to know how to stop holding grudges.


A break down

I have never had a break down. I mean, sure, have I cried into my pillow? In front of my boss? Said shit I shouldn't to my mom? Of course. But the kind of break down where you've literally just lost it and you don't care about anything or anyone and if someone looked at you from the stranger's eye they would want to call up the psych ward. I don't know how to explain it. I'm not talking about being violent or aggressive. I'm talking about having no restraint. Saying everything that people have done to hurt you without trying to say it in a way that won't hurt them. Or defame them. Or hurt their pride. Yelling. Not having to hide your pain in a pillow or the washroom. Letting everyone see your suffering. Not being ashamed of it. Not being afraid of it. Not supporting it in its hideout. I wonder why I haven't. Why do I store it all within me? Only to come out in words on a screen or in my journal that are well, still hidden away. Why am I such an accomplice in covering up for the damage they have done to me? For the broken things inside of me? When will I put it on display? I really want to. But when I open my mouth...I can't even scream.
